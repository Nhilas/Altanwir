# Altanwir
_a wordy about me_
A project started with the intent of bridging the gap between the legacy ms stack and the modern cloud data stack. I have a lot of catching up to do, but fortunately my previous workplace was clearly heading towards a lakehouse architecture. This is proving monumentally useful, because despite me not understanding much at the time I still remember a lot. Thank you Ruben, Kamil, and Alex =') special thanks to Catalina for being an angel.

## Starting Point
I had the pleasure and fortune of working with a highly dedicated and skilled team that taught me everything I know about Data Engineering within the Microsoft Ecosystem. 

My initial entry was from a support role (oltp, lots of sql) and I retained this for a bit as I transitioned into a more dev role. My BI support days included lots of data quality, and gathering an intimate knowledge of our pipelines, jobs, ETLs, databases and measures. It was vital for me to be able to explain what failed, where, why, and when.

My footing was quickly solid as I moved away from support and into dev where I used what I learned to develop (and test) ETLs and, my true interest, creating reports. I loved powerbi from the start. It's a small outlet of creativity and storytelling in an otherwise highly structured field. I dove into this full heartedly and i studied data modeling, DAX, visualisations, color schemes, and even SSRS. Still, I appreciated the Data Engineering role I maintained (we had no dedicated analyst or dashboard roles), as it let me know the data's journey end-to-end.

So my starting point is deeply rooted in the OLAP ways, having worked exclusively with structured data. I embark with excitement into the new Lakehouse world, eager to discover how everything has changed for the better.

## Strategy
I have a deep desire to understand the overall structure I operate in. Naturally, me being out of the loop and generally clueless this is quite difficult: i don't know what I don't know, how could I have any structure?

To this purpose I have created Datacraft, a gemini gem fed with instructions and knowledge aimed at the goal of not only catching me up to speed, but also revising and practicing. Through a rather excessive amount of prompting (and agentic deep research), I created the structure below.
